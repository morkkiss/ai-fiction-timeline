A faculty toolkit for Title II accessibility

Four pieces, all free. Click any card to open it. UMaine's Title II page tells you what the rule requires and the campus baseline tools. This toolkit is what you reach for next: a checker for your own course pages, a field guide that answers "what do I do first," and a routing guide for when the question isn't really about your content.

Start at UMaine's official guidance. Then use these. UMaine Title II page.

What the checker does

  • Paste your course page HTML and get an immediate accessibility audit.
  • See the gap between campus guidance and the page in front of you: what's broken, what can be fixed fast, what still needs human review.
  • Download a timestamped audit report you can keep on file while you remediate the highest-use materials first.

When the checker isn't the right answer

Need the mandate or deadline?
Use UMaine's official Title II page and the UMS accessibility policy.
Need Brightspace, Kaltura, Google, or Zoom workflow help?
CITL plus the vendor's own docs for exact platform behavior.
Need an accommodation or exemption answer?
Student Accessibility Services or the ADA Coordinator — not a generic checklist.
Need to know whose problem it is?
Use the routing guide to sort faculty, IT, vendor, labor, and remediation questions.
