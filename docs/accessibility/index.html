    Faculty Accessibility Toolkit¶

    Plain-language WCAG 2.1 AA help for University of Maine faculty preparing for the federal Title II compliance deadline.

    If you only have 5 minutes¶

    Do these three things first. They catch most failures and matter most to students.

    1. Add a one-sentence text description to every image in your Word docs, PowerPoints, and PDFs. (In Word/PowerPoint: right-click image → Edit Alt Text. In Google Docs: right-click → Alt text.)
    2. Use real Heading styles instead of bold-large-text in any document students read. (In Word: Styles panel → Heading 1, 2, 3. In Google Docs: Format → Paragraph styles.)
    3. Caption every video you ask students to watch. YouTube auto-captions are a starting point only — you must review and edit them.

    That's it. If you do nothing else this semester, do those three. You've addressed the highest-risk material and protected most students.

    → Read the full version of these three priorities

    If you have an hour¶

    Drop your actual course materials into the Checker and get specific findings.

    → Open the Checker

    The Checker accepts Word documents (.docx), HTML files, and plain text. Drop your syllabus, a lecture handout, or a course-page export, and you'll get plain-English findings with click-by-click instructions for how to fix each one in Word, Google Docs, PowerPoint, or your LMS.

    If you have a weekend¶

    Read the Field Guide format-by-format. PDFs, Word, PowerPoint, video, LMS pages, forms, audio, equations — each has its own checklist.

    → Read the Field Guide

    If a student says they can't access something¶

    Use the Routing Guide. Some accessibility questions aren't really about your file — they're about whose problem this is (your office, IT, the platform vendor, your ADA coordinator).

    → Open the Routing Guide

    About the deadline

    The federal Title II compliance deadline for digital course materials at public universities was originally April 24, 2026. As of early 2026, the federal government granted public institutions a one-year extension. The current effective deadline is April 24, 2027. The work is the same; the runway is longer.
