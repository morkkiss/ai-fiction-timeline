    Faculty Accessibility Toolkit¶

    Plain-language WCAG 2.1 Level AA resources for university faculty preparing for the federal Title II compliance deadline of April 24, 2026.

    Prepared by Morgan Talty, Department of English, University of Maine. April 2026. A colleague-to-colleague resource — not a vendor product, not an official institutional document.

    What's in this toolkit¶

    This toolkit has four pieces. Pick the one that matches what you need right now.

    • Field Guide

      Plain-language walkthrough — Word, Google Docs, PowerPoint, PDFs, video, LMS pages. Every technical term has a hover definition. Search the whole guide from any page.

      → Start here

    • Working Examples

      Side-by-side before/after demos: a course page, equations rendered with MathML, an accessible form. See what good looks like — and copy the source code.

      → See the demos

    • Interactive Checker

      For technical users with HTML to audit. Paste content, get plain-language findings with Word/Google Docs fix instructions. Runs in your browser, nothing uploaded.

      → Open the Checker

    • Routing Guide

      For when the question isn't how to fix this file but whose problem is this — campus office, vendor, contract, or labor. Two-column matrix.

      → Open the Routing Guide

    If you only have one hour¶

    Read Section 2 — The three things that matter most. Three priorities (caption your videos, add text descriptions to your images, use real heading styles) that catch most failures and affect students most directly.

    If you have a weekend¶

    Work through Section 5 — Format-specific checklists for the formats you use. PDFs, Word, PowerPoint, video, LMS pages, forms, audio, equations.

    If you teach with equations¶

    Jump straight to the equations example and Section 5f — Math, science, and equations. It's the hardest accessibility problem on most courses.

    Prepared by Morgan Talty, Department of English, University of Maine. April 2026.
